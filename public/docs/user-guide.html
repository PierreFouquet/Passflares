1. Creating Your Account

Click the Register tab on the login page and fill in:

  • Email address — used to identify your account.
  • Master Password — this is the key to all your data. Choose something long and unique. The strength indicator must reach Moderate or higher before you can register.
  • Confirm Master Password — must match exactly.
Important: Your master password cannot be reset by Passflares staff. If you forget it, your encrypted data cannot be recovered. Store it somewhere safe.

What makes a strong master password?

  • At least 12 characters
  • A mix of uppercase and lowercase letters
  • At least one number and one symbol
  • Not a word found in a dictionary

Use the built-in password generator to create a strong random password if you need inspiration.

2. Logging In

  1. Enter your email and master password on the Login tab.
  2. Click Log In.
  3. Your encryption key is derived locally from your master password — it never leaves your browser.
Session timeout: You will be logged out automatically after 5 minutes of inactivity for security. Any activity (mouse movement, typing) resets the timer.
Too many failed attempts? After 5 failed logins from the same IP address, you will be locked out for 15 minutes.

3. Creating a Vault

Vaults are encrypted containers for your passwords. You can have multiple vaults — for example, one for personal accounts and one for work.

  1. In the Vaults section, enter a name and optional description.
  2. Choose the owner:
    • Personal — only you can access it.
    • Organisation — shared with members of an organisation you admin (see Admin Guide).
  3. Click Create Vault.

The vault is immediately encrypted with your master password and saved to the server.

4. Adding Password Entries

  1. Click Load next to the vault you want to open.
  2. Fill in the Add Entry form:
    • Name — e.g. "GitHub" or "Work Email" (required).
    • Username — the login name or email (required).
    • Password — paste or generate one (required).
    • URL — the website address (optional).
    • Notes — any extra information (optional).
  3. Click Add Entry.
  4. Click Save Vault Data — entries are only written to the server when you explicitly save. Don't close the tab before saving.
Always save before leaving. Unsaved entries exist only in memory and will be lost if you close the tab, change vaults, or are logged out by inactivity.

5. Using the Password Generator

When adding an entry, click Generate Password next to the password field. A strong 16-character password containing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols will be filled in automatically.

6. Copying Credentials

Inside a loaded vault, each entry has two copy buttons:

  • Copy Username — copies the username to your clipboard immediately.
  • Show/Copy Password — briefly reveals the password on screen and copies it to your clipboard, then re-masks it after 2 seconds.

7. Searching Entries

Use the Search box inside a loaded vault to filter entries by name, username, URL, or notes. Click Clear to reset the filter and show all entries.

8. Deleting an Entry

  1. Find the entry inside the loaded vault.
  2. Click Delete Entry and confirm.
  3. Click Save Vault Data to make the deletion permanent.

9. Deleting a Vault

This cannot be undone. Deleting a vault permanently removes all entries inside it from the server.

You can delete a vault from the vault list (click Delete) or from inside a loaded vault (click Delete This Vault). You need manage permission on the vault to delete it.

10. Changing Your Master Password

Changing your master password re-encrypts all your vaults with the new key. This may take a moment if you have many entries.

  1. Click Change Master Password in the app header.
  2. Enter your current master password.
  3. Enter and confirm your new master password.
  4. Click Change Password and wait — do not close the window during this process.
All vaults are downloaded, decrypted with your old key, re-encrypted with your new key, and uploaded. Your session is updated automatically — you do not need to log in again.

11. Exporting Your Data

Click Export All Data to download a JSON file containing all your vaults in their encrypted form. This file is useless without your master password — store both the export file and your master password safely.

This export is useful as a backup. You cannot currently import the file back into Passflares.

12. Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I forget my master password?

Your data cannot be recovered. There is no password reset. This is a deliberate security feature — because the server never knows your master password, it cannot help you recover it.

Is my data backed up?

Vault data is stored in Cloudflare R2. Use the Export All Data feature to keep your own offline backup.

Can I use Passflares on my phone?

Yes — Passflares works in any modern mobile browser. The UI is responsive and adapts to smaller screens.

Why does it log me out so quickly?

The 5-minute inactivity timeout protects you if you walk away from a shared computer. Any interaction (click, keystroke, scroll) resets the timer.

Can someone else see my passwords?

No. The server only stores encrypted ciphertext. Even if the server were compromised, an attacker would need your master password to decrypt anything.
